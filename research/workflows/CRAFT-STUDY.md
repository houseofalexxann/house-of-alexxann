# Craft study — analyzing contemporary practitioners

For sources on the **contemporary astrology & public education** shelf
(CORPUS.md). These are studied for *how they work*, not *what they claim*.
A craft study never produces copy to reuse; it produces understanding the
House writes its own way from.

## Guardrails (before anything else)

- **No imitation.** The House voice is defined in
  `docs/editorial-standards/house-of-alexxann-voice.md`; craft studies
  sharpen it by contrast, never by mimicry.
- **No reproduction.** Copyrighted passages are quoted only as short cited
  excerpts inside private notes, per the standing copyright policy.
- **Doctrine firewall.** A contemporary practitioner's *interpretation* of a
  placement enters the graph only as `scholarship` or a clearly attributed
  contemporary reading — never as `doctrine`, unless they are explicitly
  citing a primary source (then cite the primary source).

## The rubric (one note per question, `notes/craft/`)

**Voice & writing**
1. Why does the writing resonate? (Name the mechanics: sentence rhythm,
   address, specificity, where it risks plainness.)
2. How are complex technical ideas made accessible without being flattened?
3. How is uncertainty discussed — hedges, framing, honesty patterns?
4. How is social and cultural context incorporated without sloganeering?
5. What makes the brand voice consistent across surfaces (app, newsletter,
   book, talk)?

**Teaching & structure**
6. How are lessons structured? (Sequence, chunk size, repetition,
   where practice lands relative to theory.)
7. How is content architected — what's free, what's gated, what's serial?

**Product & relationship**
8. How is trust built with readers — and where would it break?
9. Onboarding and subscription experience: what does the first week feel
   like? What brings people back daily?
10. What are the business-model mechanics (tiers, pricing psychology,
    retention), and which fit the House's ethics?

**Synthesis (the actual deliverable)**
11. Strengths worth learning from — as principles, not phrasings.
12. Limitations and blind spots.
13. **Openings:** what the House can do that this practitioner does not —
    where our distinct voice, evidence ladder, and citations-first posture
    create room they haven't claimed.

## Output

One intake report per study, filed in the source folder, ending with
concrete recommendations tagged to surfaces (educational design · UX ·
writing style · accessibility · community · business model · ethics), each
marked for Alexandria's accept/adapt/decline. Nothing changes on the site
from a craft study without her call.
