# The House voice — editorial standards

Phase 3 deliverable · 2026-07-13 · the standard every future sentence is
edited against. Companion to `docs/content-audit/*` and the knowledge layer's
epistemic statuses.

---

## 1 · Tone in one paragraph

Write like a sharp, warm friend who has actually read the sources — intimate
but never presumptuous, beautiful through precision rather than decoration,
confident about what is known and plain about what is not. The reader is
never broken, asleep, or waiting to be fixed; they are a capable adult being
handed good instruments. Mystery is allowed to stay mysterious; claims are
not allowed to stay vague.

## 2 · The evidence ladder (write to it, always)

Every substantive statement belongs to exactly one rung, and the sentence
should make its rung feelable. These map 1:1 onto the knowledge layer's
`EpistemicStatus`, so the standard becomes enforceable data as content
migrates:

| Rung | Knowledge-layer status | Sentence signal |
|---|---|---|
| Astronomical / mathematical fact | *(engine output — no label needed)* | State plainly. "Every year the Sun returns to its natal degree." |
| Historical doctrine | `doctrine` | "Hellenistic astrologers taught…", "Vettius Valens describes…", "Within the Jyotish tradition…" |
| Scholarly interpretation | `scholarship` | "Historians read this as…", "The available sources suggest…" |
| Contemporary practice | *(doctrine/scholarship + "practitioners today")* | "Many modern practitioners cast…", "This remains debated among astrologers." |
| Alexandria's method | `synthesis` | "In the House's approach…", "I read this as…" *(her words only — never invented)* |
| Teaching shorthand | `simplification` | "A useful shorthand:…", "Loosely put…" |
| Reflection | *(REFLECTIVE — prompts, not claims)* | "One way to sit with this placement is…", "Notice whether…" |
| Experiment | `experimental` | "We're testing this idea…", "Take this one lightly." |

**Never** dress a lower rung in a higher rung's clothes. Never flatten a
higher rung into mush ("some say…" about a hard astronomical fact).

## 3 · Voice properties (with the failure mode each guards against)

- Intimate, not presumptuous — *knows the reader's chart, not their life.*
- Intelligent, not academic — *cites when it matters, lectures never.*
- Spiritual, not promissory — *wonder yes, guaranteed outcomes no.*
- Warm, not sentimental — *"a day for the House itself" beats "you deserve rest, beautiful soul."*
- Confident, not certain — *strong verbs on facts, honest hedges on interpretation.*
- Liberatory, not sloganed — *the politics live in the framing ("your self was always going to be authored"), not in hashtags.*
- Direct, not cold — *plain sentences are allowed; not everything shimmers.*

## 4 · Prohibited patterns → preferred moves

| Never | Instead |
|---|---|
| unlock / step into your power / embark on a journey | Say what actually happens: "Cast your chart", "Read the year's timing" |
| cosmic blueprint, sacred container, highest self | The specific thing: "your natal chart", "a 90-minute reading" |
| "not just X, but Y" as a reflex | One honest clause; keep the construction only when the contrast IS the point |
| deeply personalized / transformative / comprehensive | The mechanism: "cast for your exact birth time and place" |
| deterministic readings ("you will", "this means you are") | "this placement is traditionally read as", "many people with this placement describe…" |
| hardship as cosmic assignment | Never. Hard transits get "weather" language, not "lesson you were sent" language. |
| manufactured profundity / three-adjective chains | One precise image. *"Money is your long apprenticeship."* |
| invented first-person experience for Alexandria | `[Alexandria input needed: …]` placeholder, always |
| medical / legal / financial advice framing | Interpretive-not-diagnostic frame; clinicians and professionals decide; astrology reflects |

Rationed (not banned): the aphorism formula "X is your Y", the permission
formula "You are allowed to…", bolded clauses — each earns its place by
being the *best* instance on the page, not the third.

## 5 · Weak → strong (from this site's own copy)

- Weak: *"This is your one-stop sanctuary for self-knowledge."*
  Strong: *"Western and Vedic charts, Human Design, tarot, and the library
  that explains them — one house, every door."*
- Weak: *"computed with professional-grade precision"*
  Strong: *"computed with the Swiss Ephemeris — the same calculation engine
  professional astrologers use."*
- Weak: *"…weddings, first days, surgeries scheduled with choice."*
  Strong: *"…launches, signings, weddings, first days. (Medical scheduling
  belongs to you and your clinicians; if you have options, some clients ask
  about timing among them.)"*
- Weak: *"Relocation advice — yes, where you spend your birthday matters."*
  Strong: *"Many modern practitioners cast the return for where you'll spend
  the day — a debated, fascinating question we'll look at together."*
- Already strong (do not touch): *"The sky you were born under still
  remembers you."* — poetry clearly operating as poetry.

## 6 · Calls to action

Say what happens next, in the House's accent: `Cast your chart — free` ·
`Read the historical background` · `Compare the two traditions` · `Save this
reflection` · `View your timing cycles`. Poetic verbs are allowed when the
result is visible on click ("Peek behind the veil" un-blurs the page — the
button does what it says).

## 7 · Per-surface calibration

- **Marketing pages** may sell — concretely. Price, duration, what you leave with.
- **Interpretations** carry the framing layer ("How to read these") and may
  then be intimate and vivid inside it.
- **Educational pages** cite (knowledge layer) and separate rungs visibly.
- **Functional copy** stays playful-precise — it is already the benchmark.
- **Emails** are calm and complete; every promise checkable.
- **Disclaimers** are written in the voice too — honest framing is a House
  feature, not fine print.

## 8 · The test

Before shipping any sentence, ask: *Could Alexandria say this to one person,
across a table, without flinching — and if challenged, could she say where it
comes from?* Both yeses, or keep editing.
